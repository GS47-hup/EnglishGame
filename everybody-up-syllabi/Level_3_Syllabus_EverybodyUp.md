## 📖 **UNIT 1: Things to Eat** (4 Lessons)
**Starting Page:** 6

### **Vocabulary:**
**Lesson 1 - Snacks:** gum, popcorn, peanuts, chocolate, potato chips, soda
- I want some gum. / I don't want any gum.
- He / She wants some. / doesn't want any gum.
- What do you want? / I want some gum.
- What does he / she want?
- He / She wants some gum.

**Lesson 2 - Vegetables:** carrot, onion, pepper, cabbage, potato, tomato
- Do you need any carrots?
- Yes, we do. / No, we don't.
- What do they need?
- They need a carrot. / some carrots.

## 🎯 **Course Structure Summary**

### **8 Units × 4 Lessons Each = 32 Total Lessons**

#### **UNIT 1: Things to Eat** (4 lessons)
- Snacks, vegetables, and cooking vocabulary

#### **UNIT 2: Around Town** (4 lessons)  
- Places and activities in town
- **→ ASSESSMENT: Check Up + Skills Bonus (after Unit 2)**

#### **UNIT 3: People in Town** (4 lessons)
- Occupations and illnesses

#### **UNIT 4: Getting Together** (4 lessons)
- Extended family and countries
- **→ ASSESSMENT: Check Up + Skills Bonus (after Unit 4)**

#### **UNIT 5: Fun in the Park** (4 lessons)
- Comparatives and adjectives

#### **UNIT 6: Helping Out** (4 lessons)
- Household and farm chores
- **→ ASSESSMENT: Check Up + Skills Bonus (after Unit 6)**

#### **UNIT 7: Out and About** (4 lessons)
- Past tense and weather

#### **UNIT 8: Things We Use** (4 lessons)
- School supplies and technology
- **→ ASSESSMENT: Check Up + Skills Bonus (after Unit 8)**

### **Assessment Structure:**
- **4 Assessment Points:** After Units 2, 4, 6, and 8
- **Each Assessment Includes:**
  - Check Up (skills review)
  - Skills Bonus (additional practice)

### **Recurring Elements:**
- **Stories** - Extended dialogues and conversations
- **Check Up** - Assessment pages every 2 units
- **Skills Bonus** - Additional skills practice
- **Values** - Character building components (Be healthy, Be helpful, Be thoughtful, Be nice, Be friendly, Be on time)

### **Skills Integration:**
- **Speaking** - Extended conversations and role-plays
- **Listening** - Story comprehension and detailed listening
- **Reading** - Longer texts and comprehension questions
- **Writing** - Paragraph writing and creative writing
- **Grammar** - Past tense, comparatives, possessives, there was/were
- **Vocabulary** - 200+ new words with thematic organization
